Actionable Insights & Recommendations
💸
Close the 26.4% Pay Gap
Women earn $26,225 less per year on average. Implement structured salary bands with mandatory equity reviews annually. Engineering shows the widest gap at 27.2%.
🎓
Equalize Training Access
Female employees receive 6.8 fewer training hours annually. Mandatory equal-access training programs and mentorship should be introduced to address the skill development gap.
📈
Address Promotion Bias
Male employees are 2.3% more likely to be promoted. Implement blind promotion reviews and set gender-balanced promotion targets per department and level.
🏆
Increase Leadership Representation
Only 33.1% of leadership roles are held by women vs 64.9% by men. Set a 40% female leadership target by 2027 with executive sponsorship programs and succession planning.
⚠️
Reduce Female Turnover Gap
Female employees have an 18% turnover rate vs 15.1% for males — a 2.9% gap. Exit surveys, flexible work arrangements, and parental leave equality can significantly reduce this.
🔍
Parental Leave Utilization
Only 20% of men take parental leave vs 55% of women. Normalizing shared parental leave for all genders can help reduce the career advancement gap caused by career breaks.
🌍
Hiring Pipeline Imbalance
Women make up only 42.1% of the workforce despite being 50%+ of the working population. Bias-free hiring processes, diverse interview panels, and university partnership programs are recommended.
🤖
ML Prediction Findings
The Random Forest model (79.1% accuracy) reveals Annual Salary and Bonus are the top predictors of promotion — suggesting compensation levels may reflect and reinforce existing hierarchies.
📊
Cluster-Based Interventions
Cluster 1 (High salary, low promo) shows strong salary-gender gap with 19,909 males vs 5,744 females — a 3.5:1 ratio. Targeted pay equity audits for experienced, high-earning roles are critical.
